Methodology

Pre-tournament priors — attack/defense ratings for all 48 teams calibrated from pre-tournament betting market implied probabilities. Markets aggregate more information than any single model.

Bayesian updates — after each real match, ratings are nudged toward what the scoreline implied. A team that scored more than expected gets a small attack upgrade; a team that conceded more than expected gets a small defense downgrade. Learning rate 15% per match, so early upsets don't swing the model wildly.

Monte Carlo simulation — 50,000 (Python) or 20,000 (browser) independent tournament simulations. Each sim: complete remaining group-stage matches using Poisson draws, pick top-2 + best 8 third-place teams, run a 32-team knockout. The fraction of sims each team wins is their championship probability.

Limitations — strengths are seeded from betting markets, not a paid Elo feed. The exact 3rd-place bracket placement (predetermined by FIFA) is simplified to a random shuffle among the 32 qualifiers. Injury news and squad changes are not modeled.
